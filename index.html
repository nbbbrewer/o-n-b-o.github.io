The Divine Comedy

Dante Alighieri was an Italian poet who lived in the latter half of the 13th century into the early part of the 14th. He is known for writing The Divine Comedy.

The Divine Comedy is an allegorical poem in which Dante is lead through Hell, Purgatory, and Heaven by the Roman poet Virgil. Along the way Dante describes the fate of the souls they encounter in each realm. The final part, called Paradise, is a description of Heaven as Dante interpreted it.

Dante divided his poem into three seperates parts titled for the associated realm. In the original Italian they are...

  • Inferno, for Hell
  • Purgatorio, for Purgatory
  • Paradiso, for Paradise or Heaven
